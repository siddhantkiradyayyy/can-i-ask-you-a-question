Dear Yog,

We have yet to share a moment in the same room, yet you already occupy a small chamber of my mind. There is a devious particular charm to the mystery of it all that has thrilled me. I thrill in how little I know and yet how much I can imagine. The way you speak about things that you care of lingers with me reminiscent of a page turned too soon. I do not claim to possess knowledge of that which you are, only that the idea of you feels inexplicably lovely and leaves me in delirium. If this note brings forth even the hint of a smile to your day, then I shall consider it quite a victory.

With curiosity and hope,
Siddhant

A Thought, A Walk

Permit me while I indulge in my imagination of the two of us. We walk unhurried steps through a museum, glancing at art older than our names, speaking in quiet tones because we dare not disturb their sleep. I wonder what would catch your eye. I wonder if I would notice you more than the art. I rather think I would.

Some Future Afternoon

Once you have laid down your pen that you lift in the pursuit of knowledge, and your scholarly activities have slightly paused and allowed you time to breathe, might we take a day together of promenade? I wish not for grand declarations but a quiet moment of peace and shared mirth and joy with you. You shall not be expected to charm, you shall only expected to be charmed.

A Bite and a Banter

Let us not forget a meal. For if you are famished, we would replenish ourselves with a fine delight - whether it be rich or fried. I would also replenish myself with your smile. And should the moment allow, I may offer a line or two not unlike a compliment, if only to make you blush - gently, of course.

A Proposal of Lighthearted Kind

Would you, once your schedule is yours again, accompany me to the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya for a gentle adventure? No strings, no demands, only a shared curiosity and a fondness for the soft possibility of becoming acquainted, properly and in person.



What a Lovely Thing

Thank you for reading this far. You have, in doing so, made my day a little warmer. If your reply is a yes, I shall look forward to that day with a silly little grin and a rather fluttery feeling in my chest. We are but strangers still, but what a charming thing it might be, to fix that.
